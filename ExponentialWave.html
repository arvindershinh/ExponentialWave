Let,
$\rightarrow$Function $G(t) = e^t \rightarrow Equation 1$
$\rightarrow$Function $F$ is a $\sin$ function of Length of $G(t)$,
Such that $F(Length(G(t)))=\sin (Length(G(t))) \rightarrow Equation 2$
$\rightarrow$Value of $F(Length(G(t)))$ will be plotted on a line
which is $\bot$ to tangent at point $(t, G(t))$

$\rightarrow$Our first task is to derive an equation for $Length(G(t))$

$Length(G(t))$ can be derived with following formula.
(Kindly let me know if you want to understand a rational behind this formula)
$$Length(G(t))=\int_{0}^{t} \biggl(\sqrt{{\biggl(\frac{d(G(t))}{dt}\biggr)}^2+1}\biggr)dt$$
Since $G(t) = e^t$, then $\frac{d(G(t))}{dt}=e^t$, So length Formula will become, $$Length(G(t))=\int_{0}^{t} \biggl(\sqrt{{e^{2t}}+1}\biggr)dt$$
Let $P(t)=\sqrt{{e^{2t}}+1}$, Such that $Length(G(t))=\int_{0}^{t} P(t)dt$

After evaluating an integral, we will get
$Length(G(t))=+P(t)+\frac{1}{2}\log\frac{P(t)-1}{P(t)+1}-P(0)-\frac{1}{2}\log\frac{P(0)-1}{P(0)+1} \rightarrow Equation 3$

Since we have got the equation for $Length(G(t))$,
We can evaluate $F(Length(G(t)))=\sin (Length(G(t)))$

$\rightarrow$ Our second task is to find the equation of point $(x,y)$, Such that point $(x,y)$ is present on line which is $\bot$ to tangent
at point $(t, G(t))$ and displacement of point $(x,y)$ from $(t, G(t))$ should be equal to $F(Length(G(t)))$

Since, $G(t) = e^t$, We can say point $(t, G(t))$ is same as point $(t, e^t)$

Let's first find the equation of line which is $\bot$ to tangent at point $(t, e^t)$
The slope of a tangent at $(t, e^t)$ is given by $\frac{d(e^t)}{dt}=e^t$
We know that product of slope of two lines $\bot$ to each other is $-1$, based on that we can say, slope of line $\bot$ to tangent at $(t, e^t)$ will be given by $-e^{-t}$
equation of point $(x,y)$ on line $\bot$ to tangent at $(t, e^t)$ and passing through point $(t, e^t)$ is given by,
$\frac{y-e^t}{x-t}=-e^{-t} \rightarrow Equation 4$

We also want that the dispalcement between point $(x,y)$ from $(t, e^t)$ should be equal to $F(Length(G(t)))$, equation for the same is
$\sqrt{{(x-t)}^2+{(y-e^t)}^2}=F(Length(G(t))) \rightarrow Equation 5$

After using Equation $4$ & $5$, we will get below equation for point $(x,y)$

$x=-\frac {F(Length(G(t)))}{P(t)}e^t+t$
$y=+\frac {F(Length(G(t)))}{P(t)}+e^t$

where,
$G(t) = e^t$
$P(t)=\sqrt{{e^{2t}}+1}$
$Length(G(t))=+P(t)+\frac{1}{2}\log\frac{P(t)-1}{P(t)+1}-P(0)-\frac{1}{2}\log\frac{P(0)-1}{P(0)+1}$
$F(Length(G(t)))=\sin (Length(G(t)))$

Now, using above equations, we can evaluate point $(x, y)$ for different values of $t$. After plotting points $(x, y)$ on x-y plain, we will get a very beautiful and interesting shape as can be seen below. A sin wave on exponential function.
